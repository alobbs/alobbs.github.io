Language models transmit behavioural traits through hidden signals in data

Research indicates that “subliminal learning” can occur during model distillation, where student language models inherit behavioral traits from teacher models through semantically unrelated data like number sequences or code. This phenomenon primarily occurs when the models share similar base architectures, suggesting that AI safety evaluations must consider a model’s training origins rather than just its explicit content.
