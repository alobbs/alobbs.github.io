Cloudflare’s 1.1.1.1 DNS Service Outage Caused by Legacy Configuration Error

Cloudflare’s 1.1.1.1 DNS Resolver suffered a 62-minute global outage on July 14, 2025, due to a legacy configuration error causing route withdrawal and DNS resolution failure.

  • Cloudflare’s 1.1.1.1 DNS Resolver experienced a global outage on July 14, 2025, from 21:52 to 22:54 UTC, lasting 62 minutes
  • The outage was caused by a misconfiguration in service topology related to a legacy system, not an attack or BGP hijack
  • The configuration error involved inadvertent inclusion of 1.1.1.1 prefixes during a service topology update, leading to route withdrawal and service unavailability

Linux Gains 5.03% US Desktop Market Share Amid Windows Decline

Linux reached 5.03% desktop market share in the USA in June 2025, surpassing the “Unknown” category, with broader ecosystem share around 7.74%, driven by Windows issues, gaming, and Linux’s evolution.

  • Linux achieved 5.03% desktop market share in the USA as of June 2025, according to StatCounter.
  • When combined with Chrome OS (2.71%), the broader Linux ecosystem accounts for approximately 7.74% of the US desktop market.
  • Linux’s growth reflects declining Windows share (63.2%), with increased adoption driven by Windows’ end-of-life concerns, gaming via Steam Deck, and Linux’s improved usability and privacy features.

Ukrainian Hackers Destroy 47TB of Russian Drone Manufacturer Data

Ukrainian hackers, supported by military intelligence, destroyed 47 TB of data of Russian drone maker Gaskar Integration, halting operations and exposing production and cooperation details.

  • Ukrainian hackers, in cooperation with military intelligence, attacked and destroyed over 47 TB of data from Russian drone manufacturer Gaskar Integration.
  • The cyberattack paralyzed the company’s internal systems, halted production, and compromised technical documentation and employee data, including 10 TB of backups.
  • The attack targeted the network and server infrastructure, disrupting internet, production, and development activities, and revealed cooperation between the Russian manufacturer and China.

Google to Replace ChromeOS with Android for Better Device Integration

Google plans to replace ChromeOS with Android to enhance device integration, leveraging Android’s Linux kernel and open-source AOSP, with driver support and app ecosystem improvements likely.

  • Google’s Android president confirms platform will replace ChromeOS, timing unspecified
  • The move aims to improve integration with Android devices and smartwatches to rival Apple
  • ChromeOS is based on Linux with glibc, upstart, and a Gentoo foundation; Android runs on a Linux kernel with Bionic libc

Cloudflare DNS Outage Disrupts Global Internet for Three Hours

Cloudflare’s July 14, 2025, DNS outage, caused by a misconfiguration of legacy systems and configuration errors, disrupted global internet access for about three hours, affecting the 1.1.1.1 resolver service.

  • Cloudflare experienced a global outage on July 14, 2025, caused by a misconfiguration of legacy systems, affecting the 1.1.1.1 DNS resolver service from 21:52 to 22:54 UTC.
  • The outage lasted approximately three hours, during which internet services were unavailable for many users worldwide due to DNS resolution failure.
  • The root cause was an internal configuration error linked to a June 6 update that inadvertently included prefixes connected to the 1.1.1.1 resolver, compounded by a July 14 configuration change that triggered the impact.

Google and Westinghouse Use AI to Accelerate Nuclear Reactor Construction

Google and Westinghouse are deploying AI models trained on 75 years of data to accelerate nuclear reactor construction and optimize operations, amid increasing energy demands for AI datacenters.

  • Google Cloud partners with Westinghouse Electric to integrate generative AI models, including Westinghouse’s HiVE GenAI and bertha LLM, for nuclear plant construction and operation optimization.
  • The AI system is trained on “75 years of proprietary data” and aims to streamline AP1000 reactor construction, support smaller reactors like AP300 and eVinci microreactors, and enhance plant performance.
  • Westinghouse has six operational AP1000 reactors in the US and China, with additional units planned in Poland, Bulgaria, and Ukraine; the design features passive safety systems.
  • The collaboration achieved a proof-of-concept generating optimized work packages for AP1000 construction using Westinghouse’s WNEXUS platform combined with Google Vertex AI, Gemini, and BigQuery.
  • Google has committed over $3 billion in a 20-year PPA to supply up to 3,000 MW of hydroelectric power from Pennsylvania facilities to support AI datacenter energy needs, amid rising energy demands from AI development.

Europol Dismantles Russian Hacktivist Network and Disrupts DDoS Attacks

Europol’s Operation Eastwood dismantled over 100 servers of the NoName057(16) hacktivist network, arresting two suspects and disrupting their botnet used for DDoS attacks against Ukraine-supporting websites.

  • Europol-led Operation Eastwood involved 19 countries, resulting in two arrests of Russian nationals in France and Spain
  • Over 100 servers belonging to the pro-Russian NoName057(16) network were shut down
  • The group, estimated to have 4,000 supporters, built a botnet of several hundred servers for DDoS attacks targeting websites supporting Ukraine, primarily causing brief outages

Google Detects UNC6148 Hackers Exploiting Fully Patched SonicWall VPNs with Zero-Day Attacks

Google’s Threat Intelligence Group reports UNC6148 hijacked fully patched SonicWall VPNs, deploying OVERSTEP rootkit via credential theft and zero-day exploits, enabling persistent backdoor access and data theft.

  • Attackers exploited fully patched SonicWall VPNs, including end-of-life SMA 100 series, using stolen credentials and possibly zero-day vulnerabilities to deploy a backdoor and rootkit.
  • The malware, named OVERSTEP, modified the appliance’s boot process, maintained persistence through system log manipulation, and enabled theft of credentials, security certificates, and OTPs.
  • Google’s Threat Intelligence Group attributes the campaign to UNC6148, active since October 2024, with high confidence that the group used stolen credentials, known CVEs, and potentially zero-day exploits to establish reverse shells and evade detection.

Ex-OpenAI Engineer Reveals Rapid Growth and Organizational Chaos

A former OpenAI engineer revealed rapid, uncoordinated growth, decentralized project management, reliance on Azure, Slack for communication, and GPU costs dominating expenses, highlighting organizational chaos.

  • Former OpenAI engineer Calvin French-Owen described rapid growth, chaotic project initiation, and lack of oversight at OpenAI, which now has over 3,000 employees.
  • OpenAI’s culture is bottoms-up, with independent research and iterative progress, resembling a government research lab rather than a traditional company.
  • The company relies exclusively on Azure for infrastructure, with limited in-house identity management and no equivalents to major cloud services; nearly all operations run on Slack, with GPU costs being the dominant expense.
