It targets FPGA design for iCE40 and ECP5 devices with a modern, vendor-lock-in-free interface, available on Windows with Linux/macOS builds from source. The platform also plans to add analog and mixed-signal design support in the future.

The Price of Happiness (2024)

Research shows that happiness increases linearly with the logarithm of income, meaning proportional raises yield equal happiness gains regardless of income level, while the marginal utility of additional dollars diminishes exponentially. This logarithmic relationship explains tensions in collective happiness when redistributing income and helps clarify why income inequality persists. The article also introduces the St. Petersburg paradox, where a game with infinite expected value is valued little due to this diminishing utility of money.
