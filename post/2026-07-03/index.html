P vs NP and Market Competition

A new proof shows competitive markets require P ≠ NP, as tractable collusion detection under P = NP would enable sustainable collusion, while intractable detection makes collusion unstable. Combined with prior work showing market efficiency requires P = NP, this creates a fundamental impossibility: markets cannot be both informationally efficient and competitive. Advances in artificial intelligence are shifting markets from competitiveness toward algorithmic collusion by expanding computational capabilities.

  • Markets are competitive if and only if P != NP — arxiv.org

The mask that compiles to nothing: how HotSpot’s JIT learned to reason about bits

HotSpot’s C2 JIT compiler optimizes bitwise expressions like (x << 2) & -4 by tracking “known bits” (definite zeros and ones) alongside value ranges. For x << 2, the compiler knows the bottom two bits are zero, making the subsequent AND with -4 redundant and eliminable. This approach, similar to LLVM’s KnownBits, enables reasoning about bit-level properties beyond simple range constraints.
