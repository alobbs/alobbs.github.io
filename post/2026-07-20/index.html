Claude Fable produced a counterexample to the Jacobian Conjecture

A polynomial map from ℂ³ to ℂ³ with Jacobian determinant -2 is given, mapping three distinct points to the same point, thus contradicting the Jacobian conjecture. The counterexample shows the map is not injective despite having a constant nonzero Jacobian, proving the conjecture is false.

CO2 overload in blood suggests toxic atmosphere within 50 years

Analysis of U.S. health data from 1999–2020 shows that rising atmospheric CO₂ correlates with increasing blood bicarbonate and decreasing calcium and phosphorus levels. If current trends persist, bicarbonate could reach the upper healthy limit in 50 years, and calcium and phosphorus by century’s end. The findings highlight the urgent need to reduce anthropogenic CO₂ emissions to prevent adverse health effects.
