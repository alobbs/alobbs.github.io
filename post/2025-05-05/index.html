Senate Democrats Oppose GENIUS Act Over Security and AML Concerns

Senate Democrats reversed support for the GENIUS Act, delaying stablecoin legislation; the bill requires full reserve backing, disclosures, and audits, needing bipartisan support to pass.

  • Nine Senate Democrats, including four who supported the bill, oppose the current version of the bipartisan GENIUS Act, citing issues on national security and AML provisions.
  • The bill mandates 100% reserve backing with U.S. dollars and short-term treasuries, monthly reserve disclosures, and annual audits for stablecoins with market cap over $50 billion.
  • The legislation requires 60 Senate votes to pass; with only 53 Republican seats, bipartisan support is essential, and Democrats seek stronger AML and security measures.

Danish Study Finds Minimal Impact of AI Chatbots on Wages and Jobs

A Danish study (2023–2024) shows AI chatbots had minimal impact on wages/employment, created new tasks for 8.4%, and yielded modest productivity gains, questioning AI’s immediate labor market effects.

  • A study analyzing Danish labor market data from 2023–2024 found AI chatbots had no significant impact on wages or employment, with effects under 1%
  • AI tools increased task creation for 8.4% of workers, including those not directly using chatbots, offsetting time savings
  • Reported average time savings were 2.8% (about one hour per week), with only 3–7% of productivity gains translating into higher earnings

RSAC 2025 Highlights AI, China and North Korea Cyber Threats Amid US Staffing Cuts

RSAC 2025 highlighted AI’s pervasive role in cybersecurity, China as the top cyber threat, North Korean espionage efforts, and increased cybercrime, amid US government staffing and budget reductions.

  • RSAC 2025 hosted nearly 44,000 attendees at San Francisco’s Moscone Center.
  • AI was a dominant theme, with discussions on agentic AI, security implications, and misuse in cybercrime.
  • Experts highlighted China as the primary cyber threat, with North Korean operatives actively applying for jobs and conducting espionage.
  • North Korean IT workers, including Indian nationals paid by Pyongyang, infiltrate companies; Google detected North Korean applicants, some hired.
  • AI is extensively used in cybercriminal activities, notably in fraud, social engineering, phishing, and generating fake documents.
  • FBI Deputy Assistant Director Cynthia Kaiser reported that China leads in AI-driven cybercrime, with North Korea posing a significant espionage threat.
  • US government agencies, including CISA, NSA, and DHS, had reduced presence; concerns about budget cuts and staffing shortages were prevalent.
  • Industry leaders expressed that threat activity is increasing amid government personnel reductions, emphasizing the need for heightened preparedness.
  • Amazon, CrowdStrike, and Google confirmed ongoing threat intel sharing despite federal budget and staffing cuts.
  • Experts warn that autonomous AI agents could be exploited by criminals, increasing the risk of AI mishaps and malicious use.

Microsoft Switches to Passkeys as Default Login for New Accounts

Microsoft is shifting to passkeys as the default authentication method for new accounts, aiming to enhance security and speed, and will phase out traditional passwords for consumer accounts.

  • Microsoft announced on May 1, 2025, that all new Microsoft accounts will use passkeys by default, replacing passwords.
  • The company has joined the FIDO Alliance to promote passkeys, which utilize biometric authentication, PINs, and other passwordless methods.
  • Existing users are encouraged to delete passwords and enroll passkeys via account settings; Microsoft will prioritize passwordless login options and may determine the best login method for users.
