ECC and DDR5

ECC RAM uses Hamming codes to correct single-bit errors and detect double-bit errors, with DDR4 employing a 72-bit data width. DDR5 includes on-die ECC to address higher error rates from smaller, faster memory, but it does not replace system-level ECC, and potential interactions between the two remain a concern.

SIMD for Collision

Box3D uses “wide SIMD” to process multiple edge-edge tests simultaneously in the Separating Axis Test (SAT) for convex hull collision detection, which is particularly beneficial for hulls with many edges (e.g., a 32-point boulder with 89 edges). This approach avoids the quadratic complexity bottleneck of SAT by testing one edge of hull A against four edges of hull B at once. The article presents benchmark results showing performance gains from this wide SIMD method.

  • SIMD for Collision — box2d.org

log is non-monotonous in PHP and Lua

PHP and Lua’s two-argument log functions use specialized base functions (like log10) for exact bases but compute log(x)/log(base) for others, creating a discontinuity at the boundary that can flip comparison results. This implementation artifact, not typical floating-point imprecision, breaks assumptions about consistency and undermines IEEE-754’s robustness.

  • log is non-monotonous in PHP and Lua — purplesyringa.moe
