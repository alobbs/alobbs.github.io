## 🧠 AI & Cognition

### AI boosts wages for experience-driven jobs as textbook skills fade.

Early data suggest that artificial intelligence is simultaneously automating and augmenting tasks in the labor market. Wages are rising in AI-exposed jobs that heavily rely on workers' experience and tacit knowledge, while employment in these sectors, particularly for younger workers, is declining. This indicates that AI may be substituting for entry-level roles while increasing the value of experienced workers.

- [AI boosts wages for experience-driven jobs as textbook skills fade.](https://www.dallasfed.org/research/economics/2026/0224) — *dallasfed.org*

### Evolving descriptive text of mental content from human brain activity

AI advancements are enabling scientists to decode brain activity and translate inner thoughts into text. Recent studies from Stanford and Japan have demonstrated systems that can convert imagined speech and mental imagery into written words and detailed descriptions, respectively. This technology holds promise for communication assistance for individuals with paralysis or communication impairments, and represents a significant step in brain-computer interface research.

- [Evolving descriptive text of mental content from human brain activity](https://www.bbc.com/future/article/20260226-how-ai-can-read-your-thoughts) — *bbc.com*

### Timber – Ollama for classical ML models, 336x faster than Python

Timber is a compiler that optimizes tree-based machine learning models like XGBoost and LightGBM into native C code, enabling significantly faster inference speeds—up to 336 times faster than Python. It provides a local HTTP API for serving these compiled models, eliminating the need for a Python runtime and reducing latency to the microsecond range. This tool is designed for performance-critical applications such as fraud detection and edge computing.

- [Timber – Ollama for classical ML models, 336x faster than Python](https://github.com/kossisoroyce/timber) — *github.com*

### Everett shuts down Flock camera network after judge rules footage public record

Following a court ruling classifying Flock license plate reader footage as a public record, the City of Everett, Washington, has temporarily deactivated its 68 cameras due to privacy concerns. The ruling arose from a public records request and raised fears about potential misuse of the data by criminals or stalkers. State lawmakers are currently considering a bill to exempt Flock footage from public records law, which could lead to the cameras being reactivated.

- [Everett shuts down Flock camera network after judge rules footage public record](https://www.wltx.com/article/news/nation-world/281-53d8693e-77a4-42ad-86e4-3426a30d25ae) — *wltx.com*

The tool simplifies sharing and synchronizing these notes with remote repositories, enhancing collaborative development workflows.
