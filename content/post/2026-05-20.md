### Notes on I2C

I2C is a synchronous, two-wire serial communication protocol used for connecting low-speed peripherals via SDA and SCL lines. Its open-drain design utilizes "Wired-AND" logic to facilitate passive bus arbitration among multiple controllers. In this system, controllers monitor the SDA line to detect conflicts and relinquish control if their transmitted signal is overridden by another device pulling the line LOW.
