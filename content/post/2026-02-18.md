## 📊 Business Strategy Insights

### 15 years later, Microsoft morged my diagram

A diagram on Microsoft's Learn portal was found to be an AI-generated rip-off of Vincent Driessen's 2010 Git branching model diagram, lacking proper credit or attribution. The incident highlights a lack of process and care in creating learning resources, with the author calling for simple attribution and understanding behind such actions. This is not the first time the diagram has been shared and modified, but the use of AI to generate an inferior version without crediting the original creator is widely seen as plagiarism.

- [15 years later, Microsoft morged my diagram](https://nvie.com/posts/15-years-later/) — *nvie.com*

### Amazon shares rise after 9-day slide, ending $450B+ market loss.

Amazon shares closed up 1.2% after a nine-day slide, snapping a streak that wiped out over $450 billion in market value due to concerns about the company's planned spending on artificial intelligence initiatives. The stock had fallen by nearly 18% between February and last week, amid worries about free cash flows and returns on investment. Amazon CEO Andy Jassy defended the company's capital expenditures plan, saying it will "yield strong returns."

- [Amazon shares rise after 9-day slide, ending $450B+ market loss.](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/02/17/amazon-stock-losing-streak.html) — *cnbc.com*

### Thousands of CEOs just admitted AI had no impact on employment or productivity

A new study has found that despite widespread mentions of AI in company earnings calls, most executives say it has had little impact on workplace productivity and employment over the past three years. Nearly 90% of firms surveyed reported no effect of AI on productivity or jobs, echoing Robert Solow's "productivity paradox" from 1987. This outcome contradicts promises made by economists and Big Tech founders about AI's expected impact on the workforce.

- [Thousands of CEOs just admitted AI had no impact on employment or productivity](https://fortune.com/2026/02/17/ai-productivity-paradox-ceo-study-robert-solow-information-technology-age/) — *fortune.com*

### Tesla drops 'autopilot' from Calif. marketing due to DMV fine.

Tesla has agreed to stop using the term "autopilot" in its marketing of electric vehicles in California to avoid a 30-day suspension by the state's Department of Motor Vehicles. The concession comes after Tesla was accused of overselling its cars' capabilities and deceiving consumers about their autonomous features. The DMV had previously ruled that Tesla's persistent use of "autopilot" violated state law.

### Nvidia sells last of its Arm shares for $140M

Nvidia Corp. has sold off its remaining stake in chip technology company Arm Holdings Plc, disposing of 1.1 million shares worth approximately $140 million, effectively eliminating its ownership interest in the company. The sale occurred in the fourth quarter of last year and marks the end of a five-year attempt by Nvidia to acquire Arm.

- [Nvidia sells last of its Arm shares for $140M](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-02-17/nvidia-sells-off-stake-in-arm-a-company-it-once-tried-to-buy) — *bloomberg.com*

### Qualcomm set to triumph in UK smartphone ‘patent tax’ case

The UK's Competition Appeal Tribunal is likely to find that Qualcomm did not abuse its market power in the case, allowing consumer group Which? to withdraw their complaint without receiving compensation for allegedly higher smartphone prices paid by UK consumers. The tribunal will make three findings in favor of Qualcomm, dismissing claims that the company coerced other manufacturers into paying inflated royalties. As a result, consumers who purchased Qualcomm-equipped smartphones between 2015 and 2024 are not expected to receive any financial compensation.
