### OpenAI Launches New Lockdown Mode

OpenAI has introduced "Lockdown Mode," an optional security setting designed to protect sensitive data from prompt injection attacks by limiting features such as web browsing, deep research, and automated file analysis. Additionally, the company is rolling out an active session manager to help users monitor and manage account access across various devices and browsers.

### Alzheimer's patient gets back speech, bladder control and memory in drug trial

An 80-year-old patient with advanced Alzheimer’s disease experienced temporary improvements in speech, memory, and bladder control after receiving doses of psilocybin. While the experimental treatment helped the patient regain certain lost functions, researchers noted that it did not reverse the underlying neurodegeneration.

### New Treatment for Alzheimer's and Parkinson's Discovered in Japan

Researchers at Japan's Shibaura Institute of Technology have developed a new vitamin K-based compound that significantly enhances the transformation of neural stem cells into functional neurons. This breakthrough offers a potential path for treating neurodegenerative diseases, such as Alzheimer’s and Parkinson’s, by regenerating lost brain tissue.

- [New Treatment for Alzheimer's and Parkinson's Discovered in Japan](https://www.inc.com/lucia-auerbach/future-of-brain-health-how-a-new-scientific-discovery-could-regenerate-lost-neurons/91352360) — *inc.com*

The Trump administration is working to integrate artificial intelligence into healthcare by creating an FDA regulatory fast track for digital health technologies like chatbots. While these tools could potentially be used to diagnose illnesses and prescribe medication, many physicians warn that AI could introduce new complications to patient care.
