### VHDL's Crown Jewel

VHDL ensures deterministic behavior through a "delta cycle" algorithm, which separates signal updates from process evaluations. This approach guarantees processes always observe consistent signal values, unlike Verilog where signal updates and evaluations can occur in an undefined order, potentially leading to non-deterministic results. The key distinction lies in VHDL's use of signals for communication, versus Verilog’s use of "regs" for both computation and communication.
