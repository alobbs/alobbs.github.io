### [Cloudflare's 1.1.1.1 DNS Service Outage Caused by Legacy Configuration Error](https://blog.cloudflare.com/cloudflare-1-1-1-1-incident-on-july-14-2025/)
Cloudflare's [1.1.1.1 DNS Resolver](https://one.one.one.one/) suffered a 62-minute global outage on July 14, 2025, due to a legacy configuration error causing route withdrawal and DNS resolution failure.

* Cloudflare's 1.1.1.1 DNS Resolver experienced a global outage on July 14, 2025, from 21:52 to 22:54 UTC, lasting 62 minutes
* The outage was caused by a misconfiguration in service topology related to a legacy system, not an attack or BGP hijack
* The configuration error involved inadvertent inclusion of 1.1.1.1 prefixes during a service topology update, leading to route withdrawal and service unavailability

### [Linux Gains 5.03% US Desktop Market Share Amid Windows Decline](https://ostechnix.com/linux-reaches-5-desktop-market-share-in-usa/)
Linux reached 5.03% desktop market share in the USA in June 2025, surpassing the "Unknown" category, with broader ecosystem share around 7.74%, driven by Windows issues, gaming, and Linux's evolution.

* Linux achieved 5.03% desktop market share in the USA as of June 2025, according to [StatCounter](https://gs.statcounter.com/os-market-share/desktop/united-states-of-america).
* When combined with Chrome OS (2.71%), the broader Linux ecosystem accounts for approximately 7.74% of the US desktop market.
* Linux's growth reflects declining Windows share (63.2%), with increased adoption driven by Windows' end-of-life concerns, gaming via Steam Deck, and Linux's improved usability and privacy features.

### [Cloudflare DNS Outage Disrupts Global Internet for Three Hours](https://www.theregister.com/2025/07/16/cloudflare_fesses_up_to_config/)
Cloudflare's July 14, 2025, DNS outage, caused by a misconfiguration of legacy systems and configuration errors, disrupted global internet access for about three hours, affecting the 1.1.1.1 resolver service.

* Cloudflare experienced a global outage on July 14, 2025, caused by a misconfiguration of legacy systems, affecting the 1.1.1.1 DNS resolver service from 21:52 to 22:54 UTC.
* The outage lasted approximately three hours, during which internet services were unavailable for many users worldwide due to DNS resolution failure.
* The root cause was an internal configuration error linked to a June 6 update that inadvertently included prefixes connected to the 1.1.1.1 resolver, compounded by a July 14 configuration change that triggered the impact.

### [Google and Westinghouse Use AI to Accelerate Nuclear Reactor Construction](https://www.theregister.com/2025/07/16/google_westinghouse_ai_nuclear/)
Google and Westinghouse are deploying AI models trained on 75 years of data to accelerate nuclear reactor construction and optimize operations, amid increasing energy demands for AI datacenters.

* Google Cloud partners with Westinghouse Electric to integrate generative AI models, including Westinghouse's HiVE GenAI and bertha LLM, for nuclear plant construction and operation optimization.

* The collaboration achieved a proof-of-concept generating optimized work packages for AP1000 construction using Westinghouse's WNEXUS platform combined with Google Vertex AI, Gemini, and BigQuery.
* Google has committed over $3 billion in a 20-year PPA to supply up to 3,000 MW of hydroelectric power from Pennsylvania facilities to support AI datacenter energy needs, amid rising energy demands from AI development.

### [Google Detects UNC6148 Hackers Exploiting Fully Patched SonicWall VPNs with Zero-Day Attacks](https://www.theregister.com/2025/07/16/sonicwall_vpn_hijack/)
Google’s Threat Intelligence Group reports UNC6148 hijacked fully patched SonicWall VPNs, deploying OVERSTEP rootkit via credential theft and zero-day exploits, enabling persistent backdoor access and data theft.

* Attackers exploited fully patched SonicWall VPNs, including end-of-life SMA 100 series, using stolen credentials and possibly zero-day vulnerabilities to deploy a backdoor and rootkit.
* The malware, named OVERSTEP, modified the appliance's boot process, maintained persistence through system log manipulation, and enabled theft of credentials, security certificates, and OTPs.
* Google’s Threat Intelligence Group attributes the campaign to UNC6148, active since October 2024, with high confidence that the group used stolen credentials, known CVEs, and potentially zero-day exploits to establish reverse shells and evade detection.
